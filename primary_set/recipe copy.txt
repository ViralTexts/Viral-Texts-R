Recipe :
Take an earthen vessel larger at the top than the bottom,
and in it put one pint of milk warm water, one and
a half pint of malt yeast; mix them well together, and
set it away (in winter it.should be in a warm place) until
it rises and falls again, which will be in from three to
five hours; (it may be set at night if it be wanting in
the morning ;) then put two large spoonsfull of salt into
two quarts of water, and mix it well with the above rising,
then put in it about nine pounds of flour and work
your dousm well, and set it by until it becomes light
Then make it out into loaves. The above will make four
loaves.
As some flour is dry, and other runny, the above quantity,
however will be a guide. The persons making
bread will require one fourth more salt than old and dry
flour
